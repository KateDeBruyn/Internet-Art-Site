DESIGN

Stylesheet

This stylesheet represents the theme that I've taken on for this site. It is inspired by the character I am drawing and animating for my internet art project. It's particularly inspired by her eyes. I wanted the site to complement the internet art, creating a contextual house that brings out the qualities of the artwork.

WIREFRAMES

This will be the eventual plan for the style section in terms of it's functionality. I was under the impression that along with the prototype for the internet art, this site is a prototype for it too so that's why I haven't gotten to implementing this design yet. I will attempt this design in react. I am going to introduce a side bar so that the wireframes for each page are separate from one another. I feel this will reduce the amount of scrolling users have previously been subjected to in my older sites' style pages. It will also help to organise information better.

I want the index page to be explicit so I have included precise information about the site and myself. The imagery I've used sets the context for the site as hopefully many viewers will know the connotations of an emote looking image. Emotes usually link themselves to streamers and art that surrounds streamers and online content creators.

The grid set up sets the premise for the website. I have chosen this layout as it organises information for viewers to read easily. I have matched the borders of the grid to the background so that the information looks more free flowing. This is to aid the viewers in synthesis the content they see and read.

I have made a main theory page that links to the developer blogs and the internet art page as both contain my theory progress throughout this semester. Again, this information is organised into a grid for better visability. When the user hovers over a block, it animates but slighty swelling. This is to help indicate that the block is clickable and interactive [by giving it some movement].

I wanted users to see the visual of my internet art first as it contextualises the research that follows it better - as opposed to it being the other way round. The user will see my internet art first and then can see the way in which I researched and created the piece.
