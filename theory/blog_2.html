After reading Convention and Context by Mailloux (1983), I'm trying to think about the ways in which convention and context extend literary texts and form an observation of it in design. Mailloux shows that both convention and context are relevant to shared practices in interpretation. Interpretive conventions are part of context, but contextual features functioning as interpretive constraints transcend conventions. Interpretations of literary works are shaped by the context in which they are read and understood. With the readings that I've done so far that are about Vtubers, it makes sense that the literature and discourses surrounding them cover identity and gender expression. Because the very nature and context of a virtual avatar is to act as a stand in for a real body, so the nature and context of an avatar is to supplement an identity. An avatar also contextualises an identity through its features, mannerisms, and the environment it is found in. And convention comes into play when creating these avatars - like how most Vtuber avatars have an anime style to them, many take on wolf or cat ears or incorporate some kind of animal feature into their persona. There are many animal and non-human avatars too. But convention can already be found in the Vtuber sphere and the context of which can also be identified fairly quickly.

In terms of the site, I've begun scaffolding the pages I need to include. I started wireframing and have begun to plan out my style guide. A small thing I have noticed so far is how I am organising my headings. Last year I was told that I was using my headings as stylistic choices (which Iman confirmed I wasn't but anyway) so this time round I'd like to try organise the CSS side better. Instead of making IDs unique to h1, h2, h3 and so forth, I'd like to make classes that cover big headings, main headings, and subheadings. And then use IDs for more niche ones that I'll need along the way. It's just something I've been thinking about through this design process as I'd like to have a design style that is organised and deliberate than my previous work.
