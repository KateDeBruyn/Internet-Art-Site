Developer Blog Three

I've moved on to animating the rig in Live2D and it has made me think about the design process as an iterative one. As I animate, I learn how the rig could have been drawn better or drawn to allow for more functionality. For example, an artist I watched showed their PSD file and explained how they used clipping masks in the drawing process. One way to use them is for shadows around the body so that if the character turns its head, the shadow behaves in a more natural way by moving in and out of view. Another way clipping masks can be used are in the eyes, the pupils and irises can be masks of the white of the eye so that they only exist within the boundaries of the whites of the eyes.

Live2D does have a clipping function that you can use, so it wasn't an issue that I initially had not drawn my model with a bunch of clipping masks, but it is now something I will keep in mind when I'm redrawing the model in the future. I've also made the assumption that most vtuber rigs have fringes to enable more physics that frame the face - allowing for more movement and attention to detail. The side parting that I have initially drawn appears stiff in comparison to the bouncy fringe pieces in the tutorials I'm following. So, I'm going to give Fio a haircut. What I'm trying to describe is a method to the process - an iterative one where design continuously informs design. The design methods and tools used in Live2D depend on how the model has been drawn in Photoshop (in my case), and the redrawing of the model is informed by what Live2D has shown to be capable of and how it can be used. My model can look and operate better if redrawn with certain aspects in mind, like the aforementioned hair pieces and body shadows.
